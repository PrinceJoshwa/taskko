The problem

Sales teams don't lose deals in one place. They lose them in the handoffs.

A lead comes in on one channel, gets followed up on a phone, scheduled on a calendar, and booked against inventory tracked in someone's head. Every handoff is where the gap opens.

Without Tasko

What breaks in a manual process

✕Leads sit in spreadsheets until someone remembers to call them back.
✕Site visit no-shows go unlogged, so nobody follows up on why.
✕A founder finds out a lead has gone cold only after the deal is already dead.
✕Managers can't tell which executives went quiet today until it's too late.
✕The same unit gets promised to two clients because inventory lives in one person's head.
With Tasko

What closes the gap

✓Every lead lands in one pipeline the moment it comes in, from any source.
✓Site visits are scheduled, marked done or no-show, and feed straight into follow-up.
✓Stale leads surface as a notification the day they go quiet, not weeks later.
✓Managers see executive activity and overdue follow-ups in real time, by role.
✓Inventory updates the instant a unit is held, booked or sold — one board, everyone.
